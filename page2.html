Here is the ways you can choose to get in Java Island:


From Bali


Java is just a 1-hour flight from Bali and can easily be combined with a route through Bali. If you are coming from Bali, you have two options of getting to Java. 1.Take a short 45-minute flight from Denpasar to Yogyakarta. Alternatively, you can fly into Surabaya. 2.Take a shuttle bus to the very western part of Bali where you will then catch a ferry to Banyuwangi. After landing in Java, it is a short, 15-minute ride, and you will land in the town that is the base for visiting Ijen Crater (aka Banyuwangi). To maximize your time, it is suggested one of the following routes: a) West to East: Fly to Yogyakarta, travel east, take a ferry back to Bali. b) East to West: Ferry to Banyuwangi, travel west to Yogyakarta, fly back to Bali (or your next destination).


Do not want to fly?


It is possible to take a ferry to and from Java. However, you will need more than one week to hit all the spots on this list and circle back to Banyuwangi.
















The island of Java is more than a thousand kilometers long; it is the most populous island in the Indonesian archipelago and a famous tourist destination.










When walking along Baluran and Meru Betiri Parks, you can see a lot of rare plants, songbirds, and other typical dwellers of these beautiful places.
